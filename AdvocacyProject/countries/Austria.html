Austria

Since Austria was part of a big empire until 1916, there are a lot influences especially from eastern European countries like Hungary, Czech Republic or even Turkey. Overall, the Austrian kitchen is famous for its pastries. We have numerous types of strudels, pies, cakes and rolls. This is why I want to share my favorite strudel recipe with you. Interestingly, the word strudel means something like whirl or eddy. Apple strudel is definitely one of the most typical Austrian pastries. The oldest known handwritten recipe dates back to 1696.

Strudel

Ingredients

  • Puff pastry
  • 2-3 apples
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 tbsp raisins
  • Lemon juice
  • Cinnamon
  • melted butter or oil
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup grated hazelnuts, walnuts or almonds

Instructions

  1. have the shop-bought dough to hand.
  2. Mix sliced apples, raisins, nuts with sugar, cinnamon and lemon juice
  3. Add a little bit of sugar and a generous pinch of cinnamon
  4. Pre-heat the oven to 400° F
  5. Put the breadcrumbs, apples onto the puff pastry
  6. Fold the dough at the edges and roll it. Seal the ends well.
  7. Bake according to directions on dough packaging, or until the dough is golden brown

Käselaibchen (Cheese Patties)

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups of grated cheese (Swiss preferably)
  • mixed herbs and garlic
  • 1/2 cup bacon, ham, salami, bell pepper
  • 2 cups of diced old or toasted bread, bagels etc.
  • 2 cups water or milk
  • 2 cups oil

Instructions

  1. Mix everything in a bowl, add water or milk if you need more liquid.
  2. Form into patties with hands.
  3. Fry in a pan and serve with sour cream dipping.
